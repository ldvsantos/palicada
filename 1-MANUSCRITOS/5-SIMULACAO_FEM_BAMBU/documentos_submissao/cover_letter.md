We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "Structural performance of bamboo palisades under degradation and hydro-sedimentary loading assessed via finite element analysis" for consideration in the *Journal of the Brazilian Society of Mechanical Sciences and Engineering* (*J. Braz. Soc. Mech. Sci. Eng.*).

Bamboo (*Bambusa vulgaris*) palisades are widely adopted in tropical bioengineering as permeable check dams for gully erosion control. Despite growing field evidence of their sediment-trapping efficiency, the temporal evolution of their structural integrity under progressive biodegradation coupled with hydrosedimentological loading has not been quantified. This gap impairs the design of maintenance protocols and the estimation of functional longevity, both critical for ecosystem restoration planning. The present study addresses this gap through a three-dimensional finite element model (Euler-Bernoulli beam elements, Tsai-Hill failure criterion) combining 21 time steps, three degradation scenarios, and three hydrological scenarios (567 combinations total), calibrated with two years of field monitoring data from a Plinthosol gully in northeastern Brazil.

We show that the safety factor remains above 2.7 across all 567 combinations; that vertical stakes (not horizontal culms) concentrate structural demand, consuming up to 36% of residual capacity under pessimistic degradation; and that interlaminar shear at culm–stake nodes accounts for 98% of the Tsai-Hill index in the critical element, identifying node joints as the primary maintenance target rather than cross-sectional enlargement. Importantly, sediment storage capacity reaches full saturation before any mechanical failure occurs in every scenario, establishing periodic desiltation as the intervention with the highest impact on functional longevity.

Our manuscript contributes to *J. Braz. Soc. Mech. Sci. Eng.* by presenting the first three-dimensional finite element framework with an orthotropic failure criterion (Tsai-Hill) applied to bamboo structural elements under time-varying mechanical degradation and hydrosedimentological loading — a natural composite material with well-documented anisotropic behaviour (Iwasaki et al. 2022, J. Braz. Soc. Mech. Sci. Eng.; Budhe et al. 2019, J. Braz. Soc. Mech. Sci. Eng.). The results establish structural design thresholds for bamboo bioengineering elements and quantify the relative importance of biodegradation rate versus hydrosedimentological loading as primary control variables of long-term structural safety, a question unresolved in the soil bioengineering literature.
