Dear Editors,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "Sediment storage saturation as the leading functional constraint on bamboo palisade longevity in tropical gully erosion control" for consideration in *Geotechnical and Geological Engineering*. This study presents a three-dimensional finite element framework for assessing the structural integrity of *Bambusa vulgaris* palisades used as permeable check dams in a tropical gully. Using Euler-Bernoulli beam elements and the Tsai-Hill orthotropic failure criterion, the model is calibrated with two-year field monitoring data from a Plinthosol catchment in northeastern Brazil and evaluates 567 combinations of degradation rate, hydrological scenario, and time step over a 10-year horizon.

We show that sediment storage saturates before any structural failure risk in every scenario (1.0–4.8 yr), causing hydrological scenarios to converge to the same structural demand after saturation. The material degradation rate, rather than rainfall variability, governs the safety factor trajectory, and culm–stake junctions concentrate 98 % of the failure index through interlaminar shear at nodal zones, identifying the critical inspection target for bamboo structures exposed to weathering.

The manuscript aligns with the journal's scope on geotechnical engineering, slope stability, and soil bioengineering for erosion control. Our study extends current knowledge on permeable check dam stability by coupling orthotropic material degradation with field-calibrated sediment loading in a mechanistic structural model, delivering quantitative maintenance thresholds applicable to gully rehabilitation programmes in tropical catchments.
