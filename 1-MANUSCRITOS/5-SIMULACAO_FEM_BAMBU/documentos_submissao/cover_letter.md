Dear Editors,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "Biodegradation rate as the dominant control on structural safety of bamboo palisades in gully erosion management" for consideration in the *International Journal of River Basin Management*. This study examines the structural integrity of *Bambusa vulgaris* palisades used as permeable check dams for gully erosion control in a tropical catchment. Moving beyond conventional sediment retention assessments, we propose a finite element framework (Euler-Bernoulli beams, Tsai-Hill orthotropic failure criterion) calibrated with two-year field monitoring data from a Plinthosol gully in northeastern Brazil, evaluating 567 combinations of degradation rate, hydrological scenario, and time step over a 10-year horizon.

We show that sediment storage saturates before any structural failure risk in every scenario (1.0–4.8 yr), causing the three hydrological scenarios to converge to the same structural demand after saturation; that the biodegradation rate, rather than rainfall variability, governs the safety factor beyond the sediment saturation time; and that culm–stake junctions concentrate 98% of the failure index through interlaminar shear, directing inspection resources to node zones rather than cross-sectional enlargement.

Our manuscript contributes to the journal's scope in integrated river basin management and erosion control by offering an empirically grounded, reproducible framework for scheduling maintenance of low-cost bioengineering interventions. The practical guidelines derived from the modelling (when to desilt, what to inspect, and what degradation thresholds to monitor) complement recent IJRBM contributions on check dam performance and sediment yield assessment in tropical basins.
